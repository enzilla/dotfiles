# Animation Vocabulary

Turn a vague description of motion into the precise term. Lead with the best match, give its one-line meaning, and include at most two close alternatives when they are easy to confuse. Be concise; use the glossary language rather than inventing terminology.

## Glossary

- **Fade in / Fade out** — Appearing or disappearing by changing opacity.
- **Slide in** — Entering from off-screen.
- **Scale in** — Growing from smaller to full size, often with a fade.
- **Pop in** — Appearing with a small overshoot.
- **Reveal** — Gradually uncovering content with a clip-path or mask.
- **Stagger** — Animating a group in sequence with small delays.
- **Translate / Scale / Rotate / Skew** — Moving, resizing, spinning, or slanting an element.
- **Transform origin** — The anchor from which a scale or rotation happens.
- **Origin-aware animation** — Growing a popover or related surface from its trigger.
- **Crossfade** — One state fades out as another fades in at the same position.
- **Morph** — One shape smoothly turns into another.
- **Shared element transition** — An element travels and transforms between positions.
- **Layout animation** — A changed size or position animates instead of snapping.
- **Accordion / Collapse** — A section smoothly expands or contracts.
- **Scroll reveal / Scroll-driven animation / Parallax** — Motion tied to scrolling.
- **Press feedback** — A subtle scale-down or visual response to a click or tap.
- **Rubber-banding** — Increasing resistance and snap-back past a boundary.
- **Ease-out / Ease-in-out / Linear** — Fast-then-slow, slow-fast-slow, or constant-speed timing.
- **Spring** — Physics-based motion using tension, damping, and mass rather than a fixed duration.
- **Momentum / Velocity / Interruptible animation** — Motion that carries speed and can be redirected while running.
- **Marquee / Pulse / Float** — Continuous looping, attention, or ambient motion.
- **Clip-path / Mask** — Hard-edge or soft-edge concealment and reveal.
- **Skeleton / Shimmer** — Loading placeholder with a moving sheen.
- **Jank / Dropped frame** — Visible stutter from missed frame deadlines.
- **Compositing** — GPU movement or fading without recalculating layout or paint.
- **Purposeful animation** — Motion that orients, gives feedback, or shows relationships rather than merely decorating.
- **Reduced motion** — Respecting `prefers-reduced-motion` by reducing or replacing vestibular motion.
